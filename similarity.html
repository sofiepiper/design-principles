Similarity

White Space is not Your enemy has this to say on the theory of similarity: "Our minds group things with similar properties, such as color or shape. 'Like goes with like.'"(page 62)

This means our work should be organize by what is similar. If several aspects of a design are the same color, the brain wants to put them together. If several things are done in the same style, the brain wants to put them together. Grouping similar things together satisfies the brain and gives an audience a sense of organization.

To apply this in the world around us think about when children play with blocks. Many times, they will want to group blocks of the same size or shape together. If even a small child has in instinct to do this, so will an adult. When putting shapes into a design, group them together to satisfy this basic instinct.
