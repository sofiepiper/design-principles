Closure

One of Gestalt's principles is that of closure. According to White Space is not Your Enemy, "We mentally fill in the gaps in order to complete a perceived shape."(page 62)

This principle means that a designer can get an audience to see a shape that isn't entirely outlined. The brain will fill in the parts that naturally would occur to form the shape. By using this principle, a designer does not need to exactly outline a shape they'd like to portray. Leaving gaps can add more interest and keep the viewer engaged.

For example, in the drawing above, the circle has missing parts, but any reader can tell that it is a circle. This strategy could be applied to logos and advertising, or even works of art. It makes a basic shape more unique and gives it a way to stand out.
