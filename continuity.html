Continuity

White Space is not Your Enemy states on continuity, "Our minds will continue a pattern beyond its ending points. Further, our eyes will follow the direction of a line."(page 62)

This means that a designer can lead a reader to where they want attention to be with certain lines. A designer can also form shapes with lines, and a reader will see them as the whole shape, not individual lines. By using lines with direction, a designer adds flow and interest to their work.

To apply this in the world, designers often add lines going through the all letters of a brand name, or a swirl moving from the last letter of the name to the logo of the company. These additions keep the reader following the name and provide interest. These types of lines allow for the entire word, or the word plus the logo, to flow together seamlessly.
